The old lighthouse stood at the edge of the cliff, its lamp turning slowly through the fog.
Every night the keeper climbed the spiral stairs to trim the wick and polish the glass.
Ships far out on the water watched for that single point of light before they trusted the rocks below.
Byte-pair encoding compresses common substrings so that frequent words become single tokens.
A transformer learns to predict the next token by attending to everything that came before it.
RMSNorm rescales a vector by its root mean square instead of subtracting the mean like LayerNorm does.
SwiGLU multiplies a swish-activated gate by a linear value projection inside the feed-forward block.
Attention lets each position look at every earlier position and decide how much weight to give it.
Gradients flow backward through the network, layer by layer, until every weight has been nudged.
With enough data and enough steps, even a small model starts to capture the shape of language.
